# Code Of Conduct

Open Transit RT is an independent open-source project for practical transit data tooling. Participation should be respectful, focused, and useful to agencies, operators, contributors, and maintainers.

## Expected Behavior

- Be direct and respectful.
- Keep discussion tied to the project scope.
- Assume contributors are trying to improve the repository, then verify claims with code, docs, tests, or evidence.
- Treat safety, secret handling, and evidence truthfulness as shared responsibilities.
- Disagree with decisions by explaining tradeoffs and pointing to requirements, architecture docs, or reproducible behavior.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private credentials, private logs, private portal material, personal data, or unredacted operator artifacts.
- Misrepresenting prepared packets, validator results, pilot evidence, or replay metrics as consumer acceptance, compliance, endorsement, or production proof.
- Repeatedly pushing out-of-scope work such as rider apps, payments, passenger accounts, or CAD/dispatch replacement after maintainers have redirected the discussion.

## Reporting

For conduct issues that can be discussed publicly, open a focused issue or PR comment.

For private, security-sensitive, or evidence-sensitive issues, do not open a public issue. Use the private reporting guidance in `SECURITY.md`.

Maintainers may edit, hide, or close comments and issues that expose secrets, private operator artifacts, or unsupported claims.

## Maintainer Response

Maintainers may ask for clarification, redirect an issue to a safer private channel, close off-topic discussions, or block abusive participation. Decisions should be documented when they affect project governance, architecture, release process, evidence wording, or scope.
